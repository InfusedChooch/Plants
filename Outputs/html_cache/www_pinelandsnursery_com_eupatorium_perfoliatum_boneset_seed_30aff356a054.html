    Eupatorium perfoliatum is a hardy perennial wildflower native to North America, thriving in wetlands, marshes, and damp meadows. It grows 3–4 feet tall and is easily identified by its opposite leaves that appear fused around the stem. Flat-topped clusters of small, white flowers bloom from mid to late summer, providing a striking feature in naturalized settings.

    Boneset is an excellent choice for wetland restoration projects, as it tolerates saturated soils and provides nectar for a wide range of pollinators, including bees and butterflies. It also stabilizes soil in moist areas, making it valuable for riparian and rain garden installations. Its historical use as a medicinal plant adds cultural significance to ecological     landscapes.
